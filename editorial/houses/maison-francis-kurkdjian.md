Maison Francis Kurkdjian was founded in 2009 by one of the most respected working perfumers. Kurkdjian had already composed Le Mâle for Jean Paul Gaultier (1995), Narciso Rodriguez For Her, and Acqua di Parma's Iris Nobile. The house's catalog is small, deliberate, and notably consistent. Baccarat Rouge 540 (2015) became a cultural phenomenon and arguably the most-referenced niche fragrance of the 2010s. LVMH bought the house in 2017 and Kurkdjian is now also creative director at Dior.
