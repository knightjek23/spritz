Versace's fragrance program runs through Euroitalia, which has held the license since the early 80s. The catalog leans loud — Eros, Eros Flame, Crystal Noir, Bright Crystal — and reads as exactly what you'd expect from a house known for gold Medusa heads on everything. Aimed squarely at the going-out market: bold openings, dense sweet bases, projection over subtlety. The house has had occasional moments of restraint (Pour Homme, the original Versense) but those aren't the bottles selling at the duty-free. Predictable in a way that's a feature, not a bug.
