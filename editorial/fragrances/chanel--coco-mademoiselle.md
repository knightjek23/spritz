Coco Mademoiselle was Chanel's bid to make a younger, sharper version of the original Coco — a bright orange-bergamot opening over a patchouli-rose-vanilla base. Polge built it to feel modern in 2001, and ironically that's exactly why it's now the fragrance that reads as "millennial bridesmaid." Performance is strong, projection is moderate, and the dry-down is genuinely good. If you've smelled fragrance in a corporate office in the last twenty years, you've smelled this. Familiarity isn't a flaw, just a thing to know about it.
