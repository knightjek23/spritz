Angel invented the gourmand category. Olivier Cresp's 1992 composition combined an enormous overdose of ethyl maltol (cotton-candy sweetness) with patchouli, chocolate, and red berries — and produced something so structurally weird that nobody knew what to do with it for years. Then it became one of the best-selling feminine fragrances of all time and spawned a thirty-year wave of imitators. Performance is monstrous; one spray easily lasts 12+ hours. Polarizing in the most extreme sense — half the people who smell it find it transcendent, the other half find it physically off-putting. Either way, fragrance history runs through this bottle.
