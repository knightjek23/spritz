Terre d'Hermès is Jean-Claude Ellena's defining masculine, a study in contrast: sharp citrus opening (grapefruit, orange) over a flinty, mineral vetiver-cedar base. The signature is the way it smells like wet stones in sunlight, which sounds odd but is exactly the effect Ellena was after. Performance is moderate, in keeping with Hermès's overall preference for restraint over projection. Reformulated lightly over the years; the original 2006 formula and the EDP (2009) and Parfum (2014) all hold up. This is the fragrance most often called "grown-up Sauvage," which it is, in spirit, by a decade.
