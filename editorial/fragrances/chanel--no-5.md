Chanel N°5 is the most influential fragrance ever made, and that's not a marketing claim. Ernest Beaux's 1921 composition was the first to overdose synthetic aldehydes on top of a floral base, which broke the pattern of perfumes mimicking single flowers and started the modern era of abstract perfumery. Reformulations over a century have softened it considerably; the current EDP is rounder and easier to wear than the original. That said, this is still a deeply structured, slightly cold, ambitious composition that doesn't make any concessions to current taste. People either find it unwearable or essential. Worth smelling at least once just to understand what it set in motion.
