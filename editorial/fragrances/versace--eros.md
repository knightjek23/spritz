Eros is exactly what a Versace fragrance is supposed to be: loud, sweet, immediately recognizable, designed to get noticed. Aurélien Guichard built it on mint and apple opening, with a tonka-vanilla-cedar base that reads as "candy with bite." It's been the dominant young-men's club fragrance in much of the world since 2012 and shows no sign of fading. Performance is huge. Subtlety is not the goal. Probably the most-blind-bought masculine fragrance of the last decade. People who like it really like it; people who don't are usually annoyed by how often they smell it.
