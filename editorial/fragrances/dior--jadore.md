J'adore was Calice Becker's project to build a "white floral bouquet" without committing to any single flower: ylang-ylang, jasmine, rose, and tuberose blend so smoothly that the result reads as one note rather than four. Performance is moderate, projection is gentle, and the whole thing is engineered to be agreeable to nearly everyone. The original 1999 EDT is fresher and more aldehydic; the various flankers (Voile de Parfum, EDP Infinissime, Parfum d'Eau) chase warmer or more diffusive interpretations. None of them are bad. None of them are particularly daring. That's the point.
