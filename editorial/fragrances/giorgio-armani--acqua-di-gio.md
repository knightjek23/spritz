Acqua di Giò is the most-imitated aquatic fragrance ever made. Alberto Morillas built it in 1996 on Calone, the synthetic ozone-marine molecule that defined the entire 90s aquatic boom, and the formula has been a top-five bestseller in men's fragrance for nearly thirty straight years. It smells like Mediterranean ocean spray on warm rocks: bright, salty, slightly green. Reformulated multiple times; the modern Profumo version is denser and warmer if you want a deeper take. Hard to wear wrong, hard to wear well. Familiar in a way that's reassuring or boring depending on your taste.
