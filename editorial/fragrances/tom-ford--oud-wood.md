Oud Wood was Tom Ford's gateway oud — the bottle that introduced a generation of Western buyers to the note without scaring them off with the actual leathery, animal weight that real Middle Eastern oud carries. Richard Herpin built it on a clean, sweet synthetic oud accord with cardamom, sandalwood, and rosewood. The result is "oud" in name only — a soft, polished woody fragrance that performs modestly and reads as expensive without being challenging. Genuinely good for what it is. Just don't expect it to smell like the oud you'd find at a souk in Dubai.
