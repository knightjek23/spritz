Santal 33 is the fragrance most associated with cool-leaning American cities in the 2010s. You can smell it walking through Brooklyn, the Mission, or Silver Lake on any given afternoon. Frank Voelkl built it around an Australian sandalwood and cardamom accord with a leather-iris underlay, giving it that famous "campfire and cashmere" effect. Has been called overexposed for at least eight years now and is still selling. The wax-blocked, pharmacy-bottle aesthetic is half the brand promise.
