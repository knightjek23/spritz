Y EDP is one of the better-engineered designer masculines of the late 2010s. Ropion built it on apple, ginger, and sage opening, with a base of ambergris, vetiver, and cedar. The result reads as "fresh but with weight," sitting somewhere between Sauvage's loud Ambroxan and Bleu de Chanel's restraint. Performance is solid. Polarizing only in that fragrance enthusiasts find it slightly too clean to be interesting, while casual buyers find it exactly right. The 2017 EDT is sharper and shorter-lived; most people who want this should go straight to the EDP.
