Bleu de Chanel was Jacques Polge's farewell statement before his son took over the house — an aromatic-woody fragrance built to be the exact opposite of the loud aquatics that ruled the 2000s men's market. The original EDT is fresh and slightly mineral; the EDP (2014) is denser and more vetiver-driven; the Parfum (2018) is the warmest, with sandalwood and tonka. All three are competent and most people who own Bleu own one of them and never feel the need to chase the others. Less interesting than Chanel's Les Exclusifs work but probably the most reliable masculine fragrance you can buy from a major house.
