---
name: civet
type: note
aliases: [civet absolute, civetone, synthetic civet]
family: musky
---

In tiny doses, warm and skin-like. In larger doses, frankly animal: think the back of a wet dog crossed with old honey. Civet was historically harvested from the civet cat's scent glands; today it's almost entirely synthetic (civetone). Used in trace amounts to add a living, almost dirty warmth to florals and chypres. The secret weapon in many vintage Guerlains. A long-lasting base note that becomes more skin-like as it dries down.
