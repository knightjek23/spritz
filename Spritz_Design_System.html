spritz 01 · Brand
Brand

Know what you're wearing.

Spritz is a mobile-first fragrance encyclopedia. Scan a bottle, get the full story — notes, perfumer, longevity, how to wear it. Built for the FragTok and r/fragrance audience tired of tab-juggling between Fragrantica, Reddit, and YouTube.

Voice

Confident, knowledgeable, never snobby.

Spritz writes like a friend who happens to know everything about fragrance — not a Sephora associate, not a perfume blogger doing brand work. The reader is curious and intelligent. They don't need to be sold to or talked down to.

In
Specific over evocative. Sensory comparisons that tell the reader something.
In
Tell the truth. Cult favorites can be flawed; mass-market hits can be good.
In
Length matches purpose. 30 words for notes. 100 for houses. Never padded.
Banned vocabulary
exquisite, luxurious, captivating, enchanting, sophisticated, elegant, mysterious, intoxicating, sensual, alluring, timeless, symphony, masterpiece, evocative, journey
No em dashes in copy
Use commas, periods, or break the sentence. Em dashes signal AI-generation in 2026.
No purple prose
"Smells like crushed mint stems" beats "a symphony of green freshness."

spritz 05 · Components · Hero
Components · Bottle Hero

Glassmorphism, used once.

The only place we use a frosted-glass effect is the bottle image card on the detail page. Everywhere else: clean cream surfaces, no glass.

1
Backdrop wash
Soft 24px-blurred gradient (emerald/10, brass/10, paper/40) behind the card. Provides depth the glass refracts against.
2
Frosted card
bg-cream/40 + backdrop-blur-xl + 1px ink/5 border. Radius 24px. Padding 32px vertical.
3
Bottle image
200×267px. mix-blend-multiply removes the white background of Fragrantica's product shots.
Don't replicate this elsewhere
No glass on cards, modals, nav, or other sections. Glass is the bottle's moment. Repeat it everywhere and the page loses focus.
spritz 06 · Patterns · Detail
Patterns · Detail page architecture

Encyclopedia first, dupes last.

The detail page leads with what the bottle IS, not what to buy instead. Action row sits below the title; family + notes are the visual hero; dupes and similar fragrances live below the fold.

1
Bottle Hero (glass card)
Sets the visual register. The single permitted glassmorphism use.
2
Title block
House · year · gender (mono small caps) → Name (Playfair Display 56px).
3
Action row
3 Save buttons (Own/Tried/Wishlist) + Buy CTA. Save flips to brass when active.
4
Family
Paper-background chips. Accord categories — separate concept from notes.
5
Notes pyramid
Top / Heart / Base layers. Tap-to-expand flavor descriptions for any note in editorial dictionary.
6
Encyclopedia content
Perfumer credit, house history, "How to wear it," editorial commentary (italic emerald-bordered quote).
7
Known dupes
Curated editorial + AI-generated (Pro). Source-badged at the bottom of each card.
8
Similar fragrances
Algorithmic note-vector similarity. Collapsed by default, opt-in.
spritz 07 · Anti-patterns
Anti-patterns

Things we don't do.

Most design errors are restraint failures. These are the lines this system holds.

Stark white (#FFFFFF)
Always Cream (#F4EFE6) for backgrounds. Stark white is harsh on phones and explicitly out of style in 2026.
Geometric sans-serifs (Poppins, Montserrat)
Roboto is the chosen sans. Don't substitute. Don't add a third font without a strong reason.
Glass effects beyond the bottle hero
One glass element per page maximum. Apply elsewhere and you signal "trendy" rather than "intentional."
Em dashes in user-facing copy
Use commas, periods, or break the sentence. Em dashes signal AI-generation in 2026 user perception.
Rainbow gradients
If you need ambient depth, use the bottle-hero backdrop pattern (emerald/10 + brass/10 + paper/40). Never harsh multi-color blends.
Banned vocabulary in copy
exquisite, luxurious, captivating, enchanting, sophisticated, elegant, mysterious, intoxicating, sensual, alluring, timeless, symphony, masterpiece, evocative, journey. Replace with specifics.
When in doubt, do less
Spritz's brand is "encyclopedia, beautifully presented." Restraint reads as confidence. Most design decisions get better when you remove rather than add.
