# Spritz user test protocol

**Format:** moderated 1-on-1 session, ~30 minutes.
**Setup:** participant uses Spritz on their phone via [spritz-neon.vercel.app](https://spritz-neon.vercel.app). Ideal context: they have a fragrance bottle within arm's reach (their own or yours).
**Recording:** if they consent, record audio + screen via Zoom or QuickTime. Otherwise take notes.
**Your job as moderator:** ask, observe, stay quiet. Resist the urge to explain or defend. Every "huh?" or "wait, where do I go?" is gold.

---

## Intro (read aloud, ~2 min)

> "Thanks for doing this. Spritz is an early version of a fragrance app I've been building. The goal of today is to watch you try it and hear what you think. I'm testing the app, not you. If something is confusing or broken, that's information I need. If you get stuck, try to think out loud so I can hear what you're expecting versus what you're seeing. I'll mostly stay quiet and let you explore. Cool to start?"

## Part 3: Task scenarios (~15 min)

Pick 2 or 3 of the four tasks below depending on time and what they have access to (a real bottle unlocks Task A). Read each scenario aloud as a story, not as instructions. Watch silently while they work.

### Task A: Scan a bottle (needs a physical bottle)

> "Imagine a friend just handed you this bottle and said 'try this, tell me what you think'. Use Spritz to figure out what it is and what's in it."

**During-task probes (only if they get stuck for >30 seconds):**
- 5. *"What are you trying to do right now?"*
- 6. *"What did you expect to happen when you tapped that?"*

### Task B: Browse without a bottle

> "You're curious about woody fragrances. Use Spritz to learn what 'woody' actually means and find a few examples."

**During-task probes:**
- 7. *"How are you deciding where to look?"*

### Task C: Look something up

> "You've heard people rave about Baccarat Rouge 540. Find it on Spritz and tell me what you learn about it."

**During-task probes:**
- 8. *"On a scale of 1 to 5, how confident do you feel that you understand what this fragrance smells like just from this page?"*
3, some of these words they've never heard of, don't know what certain notes smell like.


### Task D: Save something for later

> "You found a fragrance you might want to try someday. Save it so you can come back to it."

- All buttons (Owned, Tried, Wishlist) did not function properly.  Need to be routed

**During-task probes:**
- 9. *"Where did you expect to find your saved fragrances?"*
In the shelf (good)

---

## After the session: capture within 10 minutes

While it's fresh, write down:

1. **Top 3 things they struggled with** (verbatim if possible)
2. **One quote that captured their reaction** (positive or negative)
3. **One thing you'd change in the app based on this session**
4. **Their answer to question 13** (the willingness-to-pay one is the highest-signal data point)

After 3 to 5 sessions, group the findings:
- Patterns mentioned by multiple people = real signal, fix first
- One-off comments = note them, don't act yet
- Unprompted excitement = lean into those moments in your positioning

---

## Sanity checks before the first session

- [ ] Spritz loads on the participant's actual phone (different OS / browser combos can surface bugs you won't see on your iPhone)
- [ ] Camera permission prompt fires correctly
- [ ] At least one fragrance they're likely to look for has a real detail page populated (perfumer, notes, house history)
- [ ] You have a fragrance bottle on hand for Task A
- [ ] Recording works (test with a 10-second clip before they show up)
